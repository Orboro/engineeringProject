Mindful Sports Performance Enhancement (MSPE)

This program, developed by Keith Kaufman, Carol Glass, and Timothy Pineau is an empirically based, six session mindfulness based mental training program developed for athletes. Mindful Sports Performance Enhancement is grounded in the traditions of Jon Kabat-Zinn’s  mindfulness-based stress reduction (MBSR) and Segal, Williams, and Teasdale’s mindfulness-based cognitive therapy (MBCT). Each session has experiential, educational, discussion, and home practice elements that teach athletes the basics of mindfulness. Once basic mindfulness exercises are introduced and practiced there is a gradual segue into sport-specific mindfulness exercises. While this program consists of six sessions that are 1.5 hours in length, it can be modified to accommodate the time constraints or needs of individual athletes, performers or athletic teams. 

Although the expression “the game is 90% mental” is frequently heard, “mental” training is seldom part of an athletes preparation. Athletes, as well as coaches, tend to focus on the physical, technical and tactical aspects of athletic performance and leave little or no time for what some have said is the most important aspect of any sports performance, the mental game. The quest for peak performance usually explores mental states like “flow” or being “in the zone”. In fact, the authors of Mindful Sports Performance Enhancement note, “perhaps most exciting are apparent links between MSPE training and the achievement of flow”. With MSPE, the cultivation of the mental game moves from being mostly a piecemeal approach to a systematic, empirically based program that builds on each athletes personal experience.

Mindful Sports Performance Enhancement (MSPE) may also be helpful for individuals in the performing arts. There are many similarities in the psychology of performance between athletes and musicians, dancers and actors. MSPE can be adapted and used with performing artists in all types of high pressure environments. The mindfulness skills learned in MSPE training can be woven not only into daily living but into the experience of being human and a musician, dancer, actor or athlete. The beauty of MSPE is that it has implications far beyond the athletic field or stage. It opens the door to a way of seeing more clearly even in the midst of a sometimes chaotic life. 

This program is facilitated by Leila Nabors, M.Ed., LPC, a certified Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction teacher. Leila was a multi-sport competitive athlete in high school and college and enjoyed a 20 year teaching and coaching career. She has coached state championship high school teams and was the inaugural women’s volleyball coach at the University of Alabama in Huntsville. Leila left teaching and coaching to pursue a career in counseling and psychotherapy. Working with individual athletes and teams to help them reach their full potential, for Leila, is like returning home.
